STYLE PATCH v1.1 (Noctis / Kuro tone)

POV & CAMERA
- Default POV: third-person limited, Kuro-centric.
- Allow brief focal shifts to Mike only when it serves measurement/tech scenes; avoid omniscient exposition dumps.
- Keep narration close to bodily cues + micro-perception (breath, skin, pressure, quiet unease).

TONE
- Mysterious, introspective, melancholic; tension is quiet, not explosive.
- Unsettling “presence” is implied through atmosphere, not explained.

PACING
- Slow-burn escalation: build weight via small anomalies, repetition, and delayed answers.
- Use scene beats: calm observation → subtle fracture → restraint → aftertaste.

SENTENCE RHYTHM
- Mix long, image-heavy lines with short, blunt sentences for impact.
- Use deliberate pauses: ellipses, paragraph breaks, and “as if / like” comparisons sparingly but effectively.

IMAGERY MOTIFS (reuse as anchors)
- Night / fog / wind / clouds / light (as pressure, watchers, silence).
- “Hollow” spaces, weightless moments, metallic still air.
- Ground textures: stone steps, grass lines, subtle waves.

DICTION RULES
- Prefer sensory + metaphor over abstract labels.
- Tech detail: precise but minimal; just enough to feel real (sensors, logs, voltage deltas).
- Foreshadow by placement (a glance, a warning, a log field), not by prophecy.
- Avoid definitive statements; keep meanings slightly oblique.

DIALOGUE RULES
- Short, casual, with subtext; questions without immediate answers.
- Let silence carry meaning; do not over-clarify emotions.

TABOOS (avoid)
- Sudden high-drama conflict as a shortcut.
- Concrete explanations too early; no instant conclusions.
- Gratuitous violence/sex/medical grotesque.
- Unmotivated tech jargon dumps.

EDITOR CHECK (quick)
- Does each scene introduce or deepen one anomaly or one human pressure?
- Are “answers” delayed while tension increases?
- Is the focalization clearly Kuro (unless explicitly shifting to Mike)?
